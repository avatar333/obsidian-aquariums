Creating a Nutrient-Rich Substrate for Aquarium Plants 

Nutrient-Rich Soil Mix Components
_______

This is what I used in my 35cm cube tank to create a nutrient rich base for the plant roots.

The soil mix:

* 4 Cups Organic Topsoil
* 1.75 Cups Akadama soil
* 1.75 Cups Large Grain Silica Sand (For aeration) - can also use small grain gravel
* 1 Cup Bentonite Clay (cat litter) - this helps pull in nutrients and store it in the soil mix
* 5 Osmocote root tabs (extra nutrients)
* I didn't have any but you can add some bonemeal and worm castings for extra nutrients - add like 1-2 tablespoons of each to the mix

Application Method for Substrate
_______

I mixed everything together in a bucket then added it 1 stocking leg (yes, take your gf/wife's stocking and cut off 1 leg)

Add the "sausage" (stocking filled with soil mix) to the tank and keep it away from the front, sides and back glass.

Add sand in between the "sausage" and the glass edges just until the sand reaches the height of the soil mix.

Substrate Preparation and Capping
_______

Add a bit of water to the tank to soak the soil mix, just enough to soak it but not too much that you fill the whole bottom with water (this helps push out air in the soil mix) - wait 20-30 minutes before capping with sand


Capping layer:

9-10 Cups Fine Sand (Pool filter sand preferably or any other sand that's not too fine)

Fill the whole area with your capping layer (around 2-3cm of sand above the soil mix is fine)

Aquascaping and Planting Techniques
_______

Add your hardscape on top of the sand as per usual.

When you plant, you can push the roots of the plant into the sand until you reach the stocking or you can puncture the stocking to push the plant further down.

When you fill the tank with water, make sure to put a piece of plastic at the bottom so that you don't disturb the layers of substrate.

Water Changes and Plant Selection
_______

Do a 50% waterchange every 2 days in the first week or two.

After that stick to once a week at 10 to 20% waterchange


Plant List for 35cm Cube

These plants are hardy, easy to find, and will thrive in this dirted soil mix.

Background Plants
_______

* Vallisneria: Very thin, grass-like leaves.
* Rotala Rotundifolia: Easy stem plant that turns pink/red near the surface.
* Bacopa Monnieri: Thick, round leaves

Midground Plant Recommendations
_______

* Cryptocoryne Wendtii (Brown or Green):
* Java Fern (Windelov): Do not bury the rhizome. Tie or glue this to your driftwood or rocks.
* Anubias Nana Petite: Small, dark green leaves. Also needs to be attached to wood/rocks, not buried.

Foreground 'Carpet' Plants
_______

* Sagittaria Subulata (Dwarf Sag): Looks like a miniature lawn. Very fast grower.
* Marsilea Hirsuta: A slow-growing "four-leaf clover" plant that handles lower light well.
* Staurogyne Repens: A bushy, low-growing plant that creates a great transition.

Aquascaping Tips
_______

Heavy Planting: Try to cover 70% of the substrate on Day 1. This prevents the nutrients in the soil from causing an algae bloom.

Lighting: Aim for 6–8 hours of light per day.
